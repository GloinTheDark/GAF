Gravitational Acceleration Field (GAF) Theory:

A Tensor Field-Based Alternative to General Relativity

Welcome to this page dedicated to sharing my work on the Gravitational Acceleration Field (GAF) Theory. My name is Stephen James Luce, and as a semi-retired independent researcher, I have developed this theory as a novel alternative to Einstein's General Relativity.

The purpose of this page is to make the theory accessible to anyone interested in exploring new perspectives on gravity. GAF proposes gravity not as spacetime curvature, but as a mass-independent acceleration tensor field that propagates at the speed of light. It incorporates retardation effects and nonlinear self-interactions to align with empirical observations, such as Mercury's perihelion precession, gravitational lensing, and gravitational waves, while offering a field-based framework in flat spacetime. This approach avoids singularities and event horizons, potentially resolving some conceptual challenges in modern physics.
